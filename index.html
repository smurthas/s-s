I'll Ride Anywhere With You

Simon and Sarah met in 1995, after Sarah's brother Matt and Simon began playing football together at recess. Sarah can't remember exactly when they met (Simon was short and had a ponytail so he wasn't exactly on her radar), but she remembers him visiting her in college and playing flip cup at her graduation party. Simon's first memory of Sarah is when she punched him in the arm and said he needed to "toughen up" (he did). Thirteen years later they re-found each other the night before Thanksgiving in Hartford, CT.

A few weeks later Simon visited Sarah in New York for a magical flannel-filled Christmas date. That's when they knew this was the start of something special.

For the next two years they chased each other around New England, co-habitated briefly in Brooklyn, and eventually made it to San Francisco hand in hand. Their life together has been an adventure, and they are excited for the next part of the ride: marriage!

Getting In Early?

San Francisco is an incredible city with so many adventures to
be had! If you have some time before or after the wedding, here
are some of our favorite things to do:

Ferry Building/Embarcadero. Begin your stroll anywhere along the Embarcadero (a walkway that runs the length of the city next to the Bay and features great views of the Bay Bridge) and end up at the Ferry Building to check out local SF shops! more info

Sutro Baths and Lands End. If you want a little exercise head over to Lands End for a hike with amazing views of the Marin Headlands. Afterwards walk down to Sutro Baths to play along these old ruins and touch the Pacific! more info

Twin Peaks. For the best views in the city, drive up to Twin Peaks. It's amazing at sunset! more info

Take a walk on the Wild Side in the Castro. Start at 16th and Castro and walk up Castro street to find the old camera store (now a Human Rights Campaign office) that Harvey Milk launched his political career in. You will definitely see things in the Castro that you won't believe!

Mexican in the Mission. We will be spending lots of wedding time in the Mission but try to make it to 24th street (before dark!) to try authentic burritos. Any place you find will be great or use yelp.com.

Golden Gate Bridge. No trip to San Francisco is complete without stopping to see this man-made treasure. Our favorite thing to do is to rent bicycles and bike along Marina Green to get up and over the bridge. more info

Fisherman's Wharf. See the sea lions at Pier 39 bark at each other then wander further down to Fisherman's Wharf to grab a bowl of chowder and check out this water-front community. more info

Ride a Cable Car. Riding a cable car is an absolute blast. Our favorite route is along the Powell-Mason line. It drops you off by Union Square so you can do a little shopping if you're craving some hipster style. more info

Grab a Cappucino or Slice of Pizza in North Beach. North Beach is the Italian mecca of San Francisco. If you're craving pizza or canolis, head here! more info

Dim Sum in Chinatown or Ramen Noodles in Japan Town (at Suzu Noodle House). SF has a large asian immigrant community with delicious restaurants all over. In Chinatown head to Grant and Jackson and eat wherever! Suzu is our favorite Ramen place on Geary & Webster.

Get your fill of SF's hippie counter culture in Haight Ashbury. Also a great spot for Vintage clothing!
